Homework 2: Cropping and Resizing Images

Image 1 (1920 × 1080 px) RGB

Image Choice Explanation

For this particular image, I wanted to make the pink lock the center/focus point. When I took this picture, I was immediately captivated by the lock’s natural ability to stand out, with its vibrant colors clashing against the rather mundane, ordinary scenery. For my composition, I followed the golden ratio, placing the pink lock within the two right intersection points, and then utilizing negative space to keep significant attention towards my subject. For my tone/contrast, I decreased the highlights, darkened the shadows, and played around until I got an almost greenish hue. I chose these creative adjustments to play into the stale, somewhat-depressive background, and the change/hope that the lock is (seemingly) meant to represent.

Image 2 (5" × 7") CMYK

Image Choice Explanation

Similarly to the previous image, I wanted to make the “Heartbreak Bodega” poster the primary focus of my picture. When I passed by the third floor, I was intrigued by the poster’s range in colors, and how it fueled a sense of peace/joy within me. For my composition, I utilized the rule of thirds, placing the poster directly in the middle. Then, for my contrast/tone, I attempted to eliminate the strong shadows of the image, amplifying the lighting and lowering the exposure to give the picture an almost-hazy, dreamlike feel. These creative adjustments are simply meant to showcase the tranquility I felt when I saw the poster.
